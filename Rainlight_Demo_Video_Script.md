### 0:00 — Intro (title)
> "Hi, we're Team Rainlight. This is *The AI Recruiting Brain* — an explainable engine that
> ranks 100,000 candidates in about two minutes on a laptop, fully offline. It reads careers,
> it doesn't just count keywords."

### 0:08 — The dashboard
> "Here's the dashboard. On the left, our workspace: Leaderboard, Analytics, Compliance,
> Integrity Warden, Job Intent, and a Logs console."

### 0:14 — Upload (drag & drop)
> "We drag and drop a candidate pool — any `.json` or `.jsonl` file. It stages instantly and is
> ready to rank. You can also use the default 100K dataset."

### 0:22 — Configure
> "We pick the target role, then tune the *Council of Nine* — nine weighted sub-scorers — plus
> parameters like ideal experience, notice period, and the Integrity and Availability toggles."

### 0:30 — Rank
> "We hit *Rank candidates*. It retrieves with a hybrid TF-IDF + embedding index, scores the whole
> pool through the Council, gates out impossible profiles, and calibrates. Two minutes later:
> a ranked shortlist. Notice the top picks are genuine ML, AI, and search engineers —
> not keyword-stuffers."

### 0:40 — Candidate drawer
> "Click any candidate for the full story: a grounded reason, and all nine Council scores with a
> one-line rationale each — every claim traced to something real in the profile."

### 0:50 — Analytics, Compliance, Integrity
> "Analytics shows the score and experience distributions. Compliance shows disparate-impact and
> an EU AI Act audit log. The Integrity Warden lists every honeypot we excluded — with the exact
> reason it's impossible."

### 1:02 — Logs
> "And the Logs tab is our proof — every frontend and backend event, timestamped, showing exactly
> what the system did."

### 1:10 — Close
> "That's Rainlight. We read careers, verify claims, weigh availability, and explain every
> decision — at India scale, on a laptop. Thank you."
