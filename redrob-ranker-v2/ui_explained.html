Part 1

What Is On The Screen — Every Term Explained

The app is called the "AI Recruiting Brain v2.0". Think of it as a very smart AI assistant that looks at thousands of job applicants and tells you, in order, who is the best fit for a job — and why. Here is every element you see, in plain language.

① The Top Header Bar

What you see

A dark glowing banner at the top of the page that reads "THE AI RECRUITING BRAIN". It has four small coloured labels underneath it.

⚡

Version 2.0 Upgrade

This badge means we are using the latest 2026 techniques — smarter search models, faster processing, and new fairness rules. Like a phone updating from iOS 1 to iOS 2.

🛡️

EU AI Act Compliant

The European Union passed a law saying AI that helps with hiring decisions is "high-risk" and must keep records, explain its decisions, and not discriminate. This badge means our system follows those rules. This is very rare among competitors.

🧠

Explainable AI

Most AI gives you an answer but won't tell you how it got there (like a black box). "Explainable AI" means our system explains every decision — for each person it ranks, it shows exactly which reasons pushed their score up or down.

💻

Offline CPU Optimized

The system runs on a normal laptop computer without needing the internet and without a special graphics card. It processes all 100,000 applicants in about 2 minutes, which is a very tight constraint the contest enforces.

② The File Upload Area

What you see

A file upload box that says "Upload Candidate Pool (.json or .jsonl, ≤100)" and a green button that says "⚡ Load Default Challenge Sample".

📂

.json / .jsonl File Format

JSON is a standard way computers store structured information — like a spreadsheet but for computers. A .jsonl file is the same thing but with one applicant per line (useful for millions of records). The full contest pool is a 487 MB JSONL file with 100,000 applicants.

⚡

"Load Default Challenge Sample" Button

Clicking this button loads the first 50 sample applicants from the contest dataset. It's a quick way to see the system working without uploading a file. In the actual contest submission, you run it on all 100,000.

🔗

File Path Text Box

Instead of uploading, you can type the location of the file on your computer — like C:\Desktop\candidates.jsonl. This is the "path" — think of it as the address of the file on your hard drive.

Candidates Ingested

"Ingested" means "loaded and read by the AI." It simply shows how many applicant profiles were fed into the system. In the real contest run, this shows 100,000.

🍯

Honeypots Flagged

A honeypot is a fake applicant deliberately planted in the dataset with an impossible or nonsensical profile. For example: "claims 8 years of experience at a company that was founded only 3 years ago." If your AI doesn't catch these, it loses marks. Our system flagged and excluded all of them.

64%

Notice ≤ 30 Days

Notice period = how many days an applicant needs to give their current employer before they can start a new job. The job description asked for people who can join within 30 days. This shows what percentage of the top candidates meet that requirement.

🥇

Leaderboard Pick #1

Simply the ID and job title of the single best candidate the AI found. In our run this was a Staff Machine Learning Engineer — exactly the type the job description asked for.

④ The Left Sidebar — Scoring Controls

What you see

A panel on the left with sliders and checkboxes labelled with philosophical names like "Confucius: Name-Rectifier" and "Sun Tzu: Terrain Master".

These controls are the "Council of Nine" — the nine AI judges. Each slider adjusts how much weight (importance) that judge has in the final score. The sidebar lets you change priorities in real time.

👁️

Semantic Seer (Slider)

Semantic means "meaning." This judge asks: how closely does the overall meaning of the applicant's entire profile match the overall meaning of the job description? It uses AI vector mathematics — not keywords — to judge. Move the slider right to make this more important.

孔

Confucius: Name-Rectifier (Slider)

Confucius said: "the title must match the reality." This judge checks whether the applicant's current job title is actually an engineering role. Someone titled "HR Manager" who lists every AI skill is penalised here — because the title contradicts the claimed identity. This is the main anti-cheating scorer.

🗡️

Kautilya: Evidence Scout (Slider)

Kautilya (an ancient Indian advisor) said: "trust verification, never claims alone." This judge reads the work history descriptions looking for proof of real work — words like "built," "shipped," "deployed," "served 10 million users." Lists of skills are cheap; evidence of doing is expensive and hard to fake.

🎭

Japan: Mask-Piercer / Skill Trust (Slider)

In Japan, honne is your true inner feelings and tatemae is your public face. Many applicants put a fancy "public face" of skills they don't really have. This judge calculates a skill trust score — how many endorsements does the skill have? How many months did they actually use it? Low trust = likely keyword stuffing.

🥋

Shu-Ha-Ri: Path-Reader — YOE & Tenure (Slider)

Shu-Ha-Ri is a Japanese martial arts concept of mastery stages. This judge checks: does the applicant have the right number of Years of Experience (YOE)? (The job wants 5–9 years.) And are they a job-hopper — someone who changes jobs every year without building depth? The JD explicitly rejects this.

⚔️

Sun Tzu: Terrain Master — Domain & Product Co. (Slider)

Sun Tzu said: "know the ground you fight on." This judge checks: did the applicant work at a product company (like a startup or tech company building real software) vs. a services company (like Infosys or Wipro, which do outsourcing)? The job description explicitly says it doesn't want services-only backgrounds.

📅

Ideal YOE Range Slider (Sidebar Parameter)

A dual slider where you set the ideal experience band. The default is 6–8 years (the "sweet spot" from the job description). Move the handles to change what the AI treats as the ideal range.

📅

Notice Period Threshold Slider

Set the maximum number of days you're willing to wait for a candidate to join. Candidates within this number get a green badge; those above get a red badge. It doesn't eliminate anyone — just flags it.

☑️

Integrity Warden Checkbox

When ticked, the honeypot detection is active and impossible profiles are excluded from the leaderboard. When unticked, they would appear. Keep this ON — unchecking it would let fake profiles into your ranking and get you disqualified.

☑️

Availability Oracle Checkbox

When ticked, candidates who haven't logged in for months or never reply to messages get a slightly lower score. This prevents ranking a perfect-on-paper candidate who is clearly not actually looking for a job. Keep this ON.

🏆

Tab 1 — Leaderboard Ranks

The main view. Shows the top candidates in order from best fit to worst fit, as cards. Each card shows the rank number, the person's job title, their overall score, why they were ranked there, and a breakdown of all nine judge scores.

📊

Tab 2 — Score Analytics

Graphs and charts. Shows how scores are distributed (are most candidates scoring low or high?), how experience years are spread, how many came from product vs. services companies, and how each of the nine judges scored on average.

🛡️

Tab 3 — Compliance & Fairness

The legal audit tab. Shows whether the AI is unfairly preferring or excluding people from certain countries or school types. The key number is the Disparate Impact Ratio — if it's below 0.8, a protected group is being underselected and we need to investigate. Also shows the full audit log that the EU AI Act requires.

🪓

Tab 4 — Integrity Warden (Honeypot Logs)

Shows every profile that was blocked and why it was blocked. For example: "a single role lasts 200 months, exceeding the whole 8-year career — impossible." This is your proof that the AI is reading profiles intelligently, not just matching keywords.

💼

Tab 5 — Job Intent Explorer

Shows what the AI "understood" from the job description — the must-have skills, nice-to-have skills, job titles it considers valid vs. disqualifying, and the full text query passed to the search engine. This is the brain's interpretation of the job.

⑥ Inside a Candidate Card (Leaderboard Tab)

What you see inside each card

Each candidate has a card with: rank number, job title, score, status badges, a grounded reasoning box, a council breakdown, and an expandable full profile section.

#1

Rank Number

Simply the position: #1 = best fit, #100 = 100th best. The contest asks for exactly 100 candidates in order.

99

Relevance Score (the big number)

A number from 0 to 100. The higher the number, the better the fit for the job. 99 = near-perfect, 35 = weak fit. This number is calibrated (mathematically adjusted) so rank #1 always gets the highest number and rank #100 gets the lowest — and they never go in the wrong order (the contest enforces this).

✅

Status Badges (coloured tags)

Verified Title — the person's current job title is genuinely an engineering role. Product Company Exp — they worked at a product tech company, not just services. Location Match — they're based in Pune, Noida, Bangalore etc. Notice: 120d — this candidate needs 120 days, which is a concern (red). Active — they logged in within the last 30 days.

💬

Grounded Reasoning Box

A 1–2 sentence human-readable explanation of why this person was ranked here. It only mentions things that are actually in their profile (no making things up). It also mentions any concerns honestly. Example: "Senior AI Engineer with 7 yrs; verified FAISS, RAG, NLP. High-confidence fit, with one concern: 120d notice."

📊

Council of Nine Score Bars

Six coloured progress bars (one per judge): green = strong, yellow = medium, red = weak. This lets you see at a glance which dimensions made this person rank high or low. A person could score high on evidence but low on domain — the bars tell the full story.

📝

Strategic Rationales (the short text beside each bar)

Each of the nine judges writes one short sentence explaining its verdict. For example, the Mask-Piercer might say: "relevant skills are well-verified by endorsements and usage" — meaning the skill claims are backed up by real evidence.

🔍

"Detailed Profile & Career Timeline" Expander

Click this to expand a full view of the person's raw profile data, education, all their skills (verified ones highlighted in green, all others in grey), and a timeline of every job they've ever had with dates, company, and description of what they did.

✅

Verified Relevant Skills vs. All Listed Skills

Verified Relevant Skills (green pills) = skills from the job description that this person listed AND backed with real endorsements and usage. All Listed Skills (grey pills) = everything they claimed, verified or not. The difference reveals keyword stuffers immediately.

The Trap: Why Most Teams Will Fail

The sample submission provided by the contest organizers deliberately ranks an "HR Manager with 9 AI skills" at rank #1. This is the trap. Here is what it looks like — and how our system defeats it.

⚠ Why this is wrong: An HR Manager ranks resumes and manages payroll. They are NOT a Senior AI Engineer. The skills list is unverified, has no evidence of actual use, and the career history contains zero proof of ever building a ranking, retrieval, or ML system.
